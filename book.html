"The Artamonov Business" is a novel by Maxim Gorky. It tells a story of a family of serfs who have been freed after the abolition of serfdom. It spans over three generations of that family's outsprings, as the world around them undergoes drastic changes.

"The Little Witch" is a children's book by Otfried Preußler that brings up pleasant memories from my own childhood. The main character is a young — about a hundred years old — witch, who wants to become powerful to help the common people. She is kind and naive, not knowing what other witches are like. I especially liked the finale.

"Human, All Too Human" is the first book of Nietzsche that I have read. It tells about things and conclusions that are actually not too complicated, lying on the surface even. However, I still managed to get some insights into the nature of metaphysics. His way of philosophising (with a hammer) is unorthodox, of which I do approve. Personally, I liked it better than "Thus Spoke Zarathustra", partially — due to the style being more comprehensible and less dramatic.
